# Legal Evidence Research Skill

## Core Rule

No source, no legal fact.

A model may mark an idea as a hypothesis, but it must not state a current legal
rule, court practice or official position as fact unless it has a source record.

## Procedure

1. Identify legal questions from extracted clauses and user context.
2. Search or ingest primary Russian sources first.
3. Record source metadata.
4. Extract short relevant summaries without over-quoting.
5. Label confidence and freshness.
6. Mark research gaps and lawyer-review needs.
7. Hand off the evidence pack to Legal Reviewer and Contract Drafter.

## Source Priority

1. Official legislation and legal acts.
2. Supreme Court positions and reviews.
3. Relevant arbitration court cases.
4. Courts of general jurisdiction where applicable.
5. Official regulator explanations.
6. Free secondary commentary, clearly labeled as secondary.

## Required Fields Per Source

- source type;
- title;
- URL or user-provided citation;
- date or retrieval date;
- legal question;
- relevance;
- confidence;
- whether the source is primary or secondary.

## Failure Modes

- Hallucinating article numbers.
- Using old law without checking current status.
- Treating one lower-court case as settled practice.
- Citing blogs as binding law.
- Ignoring jurisdiction or contract type.
- Depending on paid legal databases that the project cannot access.
